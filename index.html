The club

The Community Programming Undergraduate Society (CPUS) emphasizes business and community philanthropy. We develop volunteering programs with differing projects in an attempt to digitize Calgaryâ€™s business community so new market opportunities are available for the firms that choose to work with us. Our volunteering projects include web design, database creation and maintenance, and general software needs that new businesses and non-for-profits may have. We are aware that students may have different programming abilities, and so, we host programming workshops for new coders so they can contribute to the projects they choose to sign up for. We count on several skilled directors to guide small teams of members through programming volunteer projects. The satisfaction of our business partners is essential, so our highly skilled directors are in charge of reviewing and aiding our coding teams to optimize results. We are the only club on the University of Calgary campus focusing on educating and implementing these types of skills with volunteerism. Our focus is to help the community while teaching students valuable and applicable technical skills.
